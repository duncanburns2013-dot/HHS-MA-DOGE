# HHS OIG — MEDICAID FRAUD CONTROL UNITS, FY2025

The HHS Office of Inspector General's annual report on the 53 state-level Medicaid Fraud Control Units (MFCUs) covers the fiscal year ending September 2025 and was published in 2026.

---

## National FY2025 figures

- **Combined recoveries:** ~$2.0 billion
  - Criminal recoveries (from convictions): **$1.3B**
  - Civil recoveries: **$706M**
- **Convictions:** **1,185**
- **Source:** [OIG MFCU FY2025 annual report](https://oig.hhs.gov/reports/all/2026/medicaid-fraud-control-units-annual-report-fiscal-year-2025/)
- **Data snapshot:** [OEI-09-26-00140 PDF](https://oig.hhs.gov/documents/evaluation/11553/OEI-09-26-00140.pdf)

## Massachusetts MFCU

The MA Medicaid Fraud Division (housed in the AG's Office) is the state's MFCU. It participated in the **National Health Care Fraud Takedown** that charged 324 defendants connected to over $14.6B in alleged fraud (see `2026-MA-INDICTMENTS.md`).

- [MA AG Medicaid Fraud Division](https://www.mass.gov/attorney-generals-medicaid-fraud-division)

## Active OIG focus areas relevant to this repo

OIG is currently evaluating:

1. **Electronic visit verification (EVV) data completeness** — directly relevant to the G0156 home-health-aide billing in `MARKUP-MATH.md`. EVV is the federal mechanism intended to verify that home visits actually occurred. OIG is auditing how states (including MA) use it for program integrity.
2. **Medicare virtual check-in / e-visit improper payments** — see [OIG report 2026](https://oig.hhs.gov/reports/all/2026/cms-could-strengthen-medicare-program-safeguards-to-prevent-and-detect-potentially-improper-payments-for-virtual-check-in-and-e-visit-services/).
3. **Payments for deceased beneficiaries** — historical OIG audits found hundreds of millions in Medicaid payments to providers for services billed under the names of deceased Medicaid enrollees. House Energy & Commerce is continuing pressure on this. ([E&C release](https://energycommerce.house.gov/posts/hhs-oig-found-hundreds-of-millions-in-medicaid-payments-for-deceased-individuals-in-a-2021-audit-republicans-are-continuing-to-crack-down-on-waste-fraud-and-abuse))

## Industry context — 2026 enforcement outlook

Outside counsel observers (Foley Hoag, Saul Ewing) frame 2026 as a "perfect storm" year for Medicaid enforcement, citing:

- The OIG MFCU report
- The Feb 2026 HHS DOGE provider-spending data release (now the largest public Medicaid dataset ever)
- Expanded congressional inquiry — see [E&C national Medicaid fraud expansion](https://energycommerce.house.gov/posts/e-and-c-leaders-expand-investigation-into-medicaid-fraud-nationwide)
- DOJ's MA-specific Health Care Fraud Unit expansion (see `DOJ-ROUTING.md`)

For this repo: the FY25 MFCU and FY26 enforcement posture mean a referral with structured data + named entities + a markup theory is landing in a more receptive environment than the same package would have in 2023–24.
