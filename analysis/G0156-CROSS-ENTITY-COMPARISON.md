## THE MATH FOR EACH ROW

Every row uses the same three steps:

```
STEP 1: G0156 billing ÷ $10.18/unit = total units purchased
STEP 2: Units ÷ 4 = aide-hours purchased
STEP 3: Aide-hours × $18.50/hr (BLS) = estimated aide wages
STEP 4: G0156 billing − aide wages = THE SPREAD
```

**The spread** is what's left after paying the aide — it covers:
- ASAP admin layer (~27.6% based on MVES 990)
- For-profit LLC overhead (FICA, workers comp, scheduling, RN supervision, insurance)
- LLC profit

For every dollar billed under G0156, roughly **45 cents** reaches the aide as wages.
The other **55 cents** is split between the nonprofit ASAP admin layer and the for-profit LLC.

## APPLYING THE MVES RATIO

The only entity where we have a full 990 breakdown is Mystic Valley. If its 27.6% admin ratio holds across the system:

| Layer | Per Dollar | Across $341M |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| Aide wages | 45.4¢ | $154.9M |
| ASAP admin layer (27.6%) | 27.6¢ | $94.1M |
| LLC overhead & profit (27.0%) | 27.0¢ | $92.0M |

**Caveat:** We only have 990 Part IX data for MVES. Other ASAPs may have different cost structures. Some may subcontract less, some more. Without pulling 990s for all 28 ASAPs, the 27.6% is an extrapolation from one entity.
