## 🔴 KEY FINDING: HIRED THE FORMER MA DEMOCRATIC PARTY CHAIRMAN AS LOBBYIST

JRI retained **Philip W. Johnston Associates, LLC** as their external lobbying firm from 2021–2025, paying **$102,000** over 5 years.

**Philip W. Johnston** (1944–2025) was one of the most politically connected figures in Massachusetts:
- **MA Secretary of Human Services** under Gov. Dukakis (1984–1991)
- **Twice elected Chairman of the MA Democratic Party** (2000, 2004)
- **Clinton appointee:** New England Director of US HHS
- **Blue Cross Blue Shield MA Board** member since 1998; Foundation Chair
- **UMass Board of Trustees** (appointed by Gov. Patrick)
- **UMass Building Authority Chairman** (2013–2017)
- State Representative (5 terms)
- Founded the **Robert F. Kennedy Children's Action Corps** — one of JRI's progenitor organizations

Johnston died April 5, 2025. His firm subsequently **merged with Smith, Costello & Crawford** — the same lobbying firm used by UMass Memorial Health Care. JRI's 2025 lobbying registration shows Johnston's firm was terminated 3/7/2025, weeks before his death.

## 4. ASSESSMENT

JRI represents a different model than the hospital systems. Rather than massive executive giving or union PAC machines, JRI operates through:

1. **A politically elite external lobbyist** ($102K to Johnston Associates — paying the former Democratic Party chairman and former EOHHS Secretary)
2. **Two dedicated individual donors** (Ward & Pond, $25K+ combined, systematically funding legislative leadership)
3. **Targeted issue-specific lobbying** (Nora Gallo on anti-trafficking legislation)

The political spending is modest compared to BMC ($454K) or UMass Memorial ($974K), but the *quality* of the connections is extraordinary — hiring the former EOHHS Secretary and state Democratic Party chairman as your lobbyist gives you access money can't buy.
